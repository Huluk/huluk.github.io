This is the second post about my project to learn Spanish. The final presentation for my bachelor’s degree and travel preparations took more time than anticipated, so that I didn’t get much done in the week before the holidays (which was from May 22 to May 30 – posts on that will follow). I was happy to see that it only took a day of repetition to get the first thousand words back into my head. In the last week I extensively used the duolingo Spanish course. That one is really good and is very easy to get into. I installed the duolingo app on my mother’s phone with an English course for German speakers and she really likes the handling, too. The courses are mostly based on translation tasks, whereas courses which only teach in the target language are purportedly better for acchieving fluency. Nonetheless it would be hard to find a higher quality online course, and much more a free one.

Now, the goal was to learn the grammar and get into conversations. The former I did, at least superficially. The latter not so much. Realistically I will complete the duolingo course tomorrow and will have much to do in the following days. maybe I can fit in some conversation over Skype, but motivation will probably be low.

Reviewing the learning process, I’m surprised how easy everything is. There are many verb tenses to learn and I find the rules for object placing very confusing, but apart from that there haven’t been any major difficulties. I’m still waiting for the shoe to drop when I reach to the subjunctive forms and special kinds of sub-clauses, should such things exist ;)
