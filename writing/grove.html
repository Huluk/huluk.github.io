The Grove

04 Jan 2015 — posted in writing

Birch grove. Two trees in front of you, one left and two more to the sides. Behind them fields of wheat, glowing yellow and ripe into the lightly clouded autumn skies. The morning sun shines through the translucent leaves and lets you experience an intense play of light and shadow between weeds and birches. To the right a raven lets his call sound over the peaceful field. None but you and him are present, each ignoring the other with benevolent indifference. An uncaringness as can only be offered to life with such different aspirations as between man and bird.

With your left foot you nearly stumble over a rectangular stone, two by four handspans on ground and one in height. A curious hollow shape carved in the middle, giving the impression of an ancient ritual place. A druid would certainly know the magic of this copse, but today it simply invites the lonely traveller to rest sometime, just a few steps away from the road behind you.

You realize that you hardly remember the aim of your travels, even the existence of the road. You get the feeling that you have forgotten something, and while you turn back to the road a darkness dawns on you, both in the rising of a thought and in the corner of your eye. The road! You have forgotten the road and now its world has forgotten you.

The darkness you sense is the shadow of the forest before you, the innumerous amount of pine trees stretching to infinity. You know there only ever were a handful of them between you and the road, but now their dark green needles stand mocking and merciless, amplifying your desperation.

The raven cries again and blight befalls the trees. You never close your eyes, but somehow the change in your environment escapes your perception: Three barren pines stand before a batch of dirt. Dirt and puddles – both quite familiar to your eye – stretching from horizon to horizon, guiding your gaze through the golden fields of wheat. The raven blinks at you, turns, and flies away.
