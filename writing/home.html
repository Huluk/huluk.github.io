You leave the cosy hut and go outside into the cold. A few steps through thigh-high snow and darkness, then your eyes get used to the dimm moon light and you see the mountains rising up even higher than your already elevated position. The wind blows clouds of snow from the exposed cliffs on the right of each mountain. It’s the same snow that gets blown on your face and bites into your right cheek, while the left one only cools out from wind alone. Every other blow lifts part of your coat and chills your intestines. Your right boot has sunken in too deep a snow pile; the usual wetness in the lower inches of your jeans now continues to your right ankle and beyond. Your lips form a smile as your gaze sweeps over the mountaintops. Now at last, the world around you perfectly mirrors the world within.
